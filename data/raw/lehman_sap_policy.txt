FEDERAL SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP) — LEHMAN COLLEGE

DEADLINE: THE DEADLINE TO SUBMIT A SAP APPEAL FOR SPRING 2026 SEMESTER WAS TUESDAY 05/26/2026.

HOW TO SUBMIT AN APPEAL:
- Undergraduate students: submit electronic SAP appeal at lehman.smapply.io/prog/undergraduate_appeals/
- Graduate students: submit typed written appeal via email to Takiyah.Ali@lehman.cuny.edu

WHAT IS SAP?
Federal regulations require Lehman College to review the Satisfactory Academic Progress of all enrolled students. Review determines eligibility to receive federal student aid.

SAP is defined as:
1. Achieving and maintaining at least a 2.0 cumulative GPA (graduate students: 3.0 GPA)
   - 5-12 attempted credits: requires at least 1.5 GPA
   - 13-24 attempted credits: requires at least 1.75 GPA
   - 25+ attempted credits: requires at least 2.0 GPA

2. Accumulating credits toward graduation per CUNY SAP Regulations:
   - After 15 credits attempted: 0 earned credits required
   - After 30 credits attempted: 5 earned credits required
   - After 45 credits attempted: 16 earned credits required
   - After 60 credits attempted: 27 earned credits required
   - After 75 credits attempted: 42 earned credits required
   - After 90 credits attempted: 50 earned credits required
   - After 105 credits attempted: 63 earned credits required
   - After 120 credits attempted: 72 earned credits required
   - After 150 credits attempted: 95 earned credits required
   - After 180 credits attempted: 117 earned credits required

3. Attempting no more than 180 credits total (graduate: depends on program length)

ANNUAL SAP REVIEW:
Each student's academic record is reviewed at the end of the spring term. All courses on the permanent academic record AND all transfer credits are included in the review.

For SAP inquiries: sap.finaid@lehman.cuny.edu — include full name, EMPLID, contact info. Allow 5-7 business days for response.

FINANCIAL AID SUSPENSION:
Failure to satisfy any SAP criteria results in suspension of federal student aid eligibility. Aid remains suspended until the student's academic progress brings them back into compliance.

RIGHT TO APPEAL:
If financial aid is suspended, notification is sent with information about the right to appeal for reinstatement.

THE SAP APPEAL MUST INCLUDE:
- Detailed explanation of mitigating circumstances (personal illness/injury, family illness/death, loss of employment, academic program changes)
- Supporting documentation (medical, death certificate, court/legal records, etc.)
- Discussion of changes in circumstances and personal adjustments that will help maintain SAP in the future
- Academic plan for achieving and maintaining future SAP requirements

FINANCIAL AID PROBATION:
If an appeal is granted, the student is placed on financial aid probation and federal aid is reinstated for ONE semester. Record is reviewed at end of probationary semester.

BACK-TO-BACK APPEALS:
There is no limit on number of SAP appeals, but "back-to-back" appeals CANNOT be granted by federal regulation if the student failed to satisfy conditions of an immediately preceding appeal.

RE-ESTABLISHING ELIGIBILITY:
- A SAP review can be requested after any term in which aid was suspended.
- Mere passage of time alone is NOT sufficient to restore eligibility.
- Students may restore eligibility by attending without using federal aid until they return to compliance.
