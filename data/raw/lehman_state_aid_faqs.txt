STATE AID FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — LEHMAN COLLEGE
Contact for TAP/Excelsior/Dream Act questions: tap.audit@lehman.cuny.edu

TUITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (TAP) FAQs:

Q: How can I get TAP if I accumulated 60 credits and declared a major past the deadline?
A: Once a student fails to declare a major on or before the deadline, they lose TAP for that particular semester. There are NO exceptions.

Q: How long does it take to process my TAP application?
A: Processing may take 3 to 5 weeks. During busy periods, processing times for "Requests for Information" and "Change Forms" may vary depending on income verification or residency review. Your award should be considered an estimate until your college certifies eligibility.

Q: What does it mean to be a "NYS resident" for TAP?
A: You must consider NYS your permanent home AND have resided in New York State for 12 continuous months prior to the term for which the award is being sought. Must be a U.S. citizen, qualified paroled refugee, or permanent resident alien.

Q: Is a home-schooled student eligible for state aid?
A: Yes, with a letter from the District Superintendent, or by passing an approved ATB Exam or GED Exam.

Q: What if I have an IEP diploma instead of a regular diploma?
A: Students with IEP diplomas must pass an ATB Exam. IEP diplomas are NOT equivalent to a local or Regents Diploma.

Q: What if I have a high school diploma from outside the United States?
A: Effective July 1, 2006, students without a U.S. High School Diploma must have passed an approved ATB exam.

EXCELSIOR SCHOLARSHIP FAQs:

Q: Can I get Excelsior if I already have a certificate?
A: Yes. Applicants who have earned a certificate can receive an Excelsior award to pursue an associate or bachelor's degree, provided they immediately enroll in a two- or four-year program upon completion of the certificate, and all credits have been accepted by the receiving college.

Q: What is the income limit for Excelsior?
A: Household federal adjusted gross income can total up to $125,000 to be eligible.

AID FOR PART-TIME STUDY (APTS):

Q: What is Aid for Part-Time Study?
A: APTS is a New York State grant program for students pursuing a degree part-time. It is money paid directly to the school for tuition only.

Q: How many semesters can I receive APTS?
A: One semester of APTS counts as one-half a semester of TAP. You may receive up to 16 semesters of APTS as a bachelor's degree student, but no more than your remaining undergraduate TAP eligibility allows.

SENATOR JOSÉ PERALTA NYS DREAM ACT:

Q: What is the NYS DREAM Act?
A: Gives undocumented and other students access to New York State-administered student financial aid. To be eligible:
- Must have attended a NYS high school for at least 2 years AND received a NYS diploma or equivalency
- OR qualified for in-state tuition at SUNY or CUNY
- Must apply within 5 years of NYS diploma (undergraduate) or 10 years (graduate)
- Must have one of these statuses: T-Visa, U-Visa, Temporary Protected Status, without lawful immigration status, U.S. citizen with permanent home outside NYS, paroled refugee with permanent home outside NYS, or Permanent Resident

Q: Can my college share my immigration status?
A: No. Colleges are prohibited by law from reporting a student's immigration status without the student's consent.
