SPECIAL/UNUSUAL CIRCUMSTANCES — LEHMAN COLLEGE FINANCIAL AID

Students may request a review of their financial aid eligibility based on special or unusual circumstances that are not reflected on the FAFSA.

WHEN TO REQUEST A SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES REVIEW:
- Significant change in family income (job loss, reduced hours, retirement, divorce/separation)
- Death of a parent or spouse
- Unusual medical or dental expenses not covered by insurance
- Natural disaster affecting family finances
- Loss of untaxed income or benefits
- Student or parent became disabled
- Unusually high dependent care expenses

HOW TO REQUEST A REVIEW:
Contact the Financial Aid Office directly. You will need to submit a written explanation of your circumstances and supporting documentation.

DEPENDENCY OVERRIDE:
In certain situations, the Financial Aid Office may be able to override the dependency status on your FAFSA. This is typically considered for students who are:
- Victims of abuse or neglect
- Unable to contact parents
- In situations where parental contact would put the student at risk

Note: Divorced/separated parents, parent refusal to complete FAFSA, and parents not claiming student on taxes are NOT valid reasons for a dependency override.

PROFESSIONAL JUDGMENT:
Financial Aid Administrators have the authority to use "professional judgment" to adjust your financial aid eligibility based on documented special circumstances. Each case is evaluated individually.

WHAT TO BRING:
- Written statement explaining your situation
- Supporting documentation (tax documents, termination letters, medical bills, death certificates, etc.)
- Any additional forms requested by the Financial Aid Office
