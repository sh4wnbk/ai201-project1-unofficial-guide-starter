WITHDRAWALS AND THE RETURN OF FEDERAL FINANCIAL AID — LEHMAN COLLEGE

IMPORTANT: Students who stop attending all of their classes or withdraw from all courses may owe money back to the federal government and/or to Lehman College.

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU WITHDRAW:
When a student withdraws from all classes before completing 60% of the semester, federal regulations require the college to calculate how much federal aid was "earned." The unearned portion must be returned to the federal aid programs.

The calculation is based on the percentage of the semester completed:
- If you complete less than 60% of the semester, you must return a portion of your federal aid
- If you complete 60% or more of the semester, you have earned all of your federal aid for that period

AID THAT MAY NEED TO BE RETURNED:
- Federal Pell Grant
- Federal SEOG (Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant)
- Federal Direct Loans (Subsidized and Unsubsidized)
- Federal PLUS Loans

ORDER OF RETURN:
Funds must be returned in a specific order: Unsubsidized Direct Loan, Subsidized Direct Loan, PLUS loans, Pell Grant, SEOG, other federal aid.

CONSEQUENCES OF WITHDRAWAL:
- You may owe money to Lehman College and/or the federal government
- Your financial aid for future semesters may be affected
- Your SAP status may be impacted
- You may lose TAP eligibility for that semester

UNOFFICIAL WITHDRAWALS:
If you stop attending class but do not officially withdraw, you are considered an "unofficial withdrawal." The college will use your last date of attendance to calculate the return of federal aid.

BEFORE WITHDRAWING — CONSULT THE FINANCIAL AID OFFICE:
Always speak with a financial aid counselor BEFORE withdrawing from all courses. The consequences extend beyond one semester and can affect future aid eligibility.
