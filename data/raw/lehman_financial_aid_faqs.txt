FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — LEHMAN COLLEGE FINANCIAL AID

WHAT DO I NEED TO DO TO GET FINANCIAL AID?
The application process for almost all federal, state, local and college financial aid programs begins with filing a FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid). Through the FAFSA you will receive consideration for Pell Grant, campus-based aid and begin the TAP application process.

HOW DO I APPLY?
- File online at studentaid.gov using your FSA ID. If you are a dependent student, one of your parents will also need an FSA ID.
- Enter Lehman College's Federal Title IV code: 007022
- If you previously attended a different college, make sure to include Lehman's school code 007022 on your FAFSA.

DEADLINES
The FAFSA becomes available on October 1st each year. It uses income data from the "prior-prior" year (e.g., FAFSA for 2024-2025 uses 2022 income data). File as early as possible before registration. College Work-Study is first-come, first-served — funding is limited.

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT?
After submitting your FAFSA, you will receive a Student Aid Report (SAR). Look for your Expected Family Contribution (EFC) or Student Aid Index (SAI) — beginning with the 2024-2025 FAFSA, EFC was replaced by SAI.

MAKE CORRECTIONS: Log in and click "Make FAFSA Corrections." Corrections process in 3-5 business days. Do not make changes if you've already submitted documentation to the Financial Aid Office — they will make changes for you.

CHECK CUNYFIRST: Pay close attention to your TO DO list in CUNYfirst. Alerts will indicate what documentation must be submitted. Common SAR flags:
- "Your school will ask you to provide certain financial documents..."
- "The name you reported on your application doesn't match..."
- "The Department of Homeland Security did not confirm..."
- "The Social Security Administration (SSA) did not confirm..."
- "Your parent's total income appears to be unusually low."

HOW TO VIEW FINANCIAL AID IN CUNYFIRST:
1. Log on to CUNYfirst and click on the Student Center
2. Click on Financial Aid
3. Click the correct Aid Year
4. Review your Award Summary

APPLYING FOR TAP:
If you are a New York State resident, you may be eligible for TAP. After completing FAFSA, look for the "New York Residents" section link at the bottom — it takes you to the HESC website to complete a TAP application. 
Lehman College TAP CODE: 1412 (different from FAFSA code)

NEED ANALYSIS FORMULA:
Cost of Attendance - EFC - Federal Pell Grant Eligibility - Aid from Other Sources = Financial Need

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA (must meet all):
- High school diploma or GED
- Working toward a degree or certificate (matriculated student)
- Enrolled in an eligible program
- U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen
- Valid Social Security Number
- Register with Selective Service if required
- Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress and Enrollment Status

WARNING — DROPPING OR WITHDRAWING FROM CLASSES:
Students who drop or withdraw from classes may lose part or all of that semester's financial aid. Consequences can include:
- Increased out-of-pocket payments toward tuition
- Having to repay received funds to the U.S. Department of Education
- Loss of part or all of the following semester's financial aid
- A student loan being forced into repayment
ALWAYS consult a financial aid counselor before changing enrollment status.

APPLICATION RESPONSES:
- TAP Award Certificate: notifies you of TAP eligibility. Keep it — do not submit to Financial Aid Office. Your TAP award is sent directly to Lehman's computer file.
- CUNY Financial Aid Award Letter: emailed to your college email. Shows TAP, Pell, Work-Study, and other aid amounts.
